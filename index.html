What is CanSat?

CanSat is an international student competition organized by the American Astronautical Society (AAS) and American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) that challenges students to design, build, and launch a simulated satellite.

The Concept

A CanSat ("Can Satellite") is a simulation of a real satellite, integrated within the volume and shape of a soft drink can. The challenge is to fit all the major subsystems found in a satellite into this minimal volume.

Mission Profile

The CanSat is launched to an altitude of about 1km by a rocket or balloon. During descent, it must perform a scientific mission and land safely. Teams must design both the satellite and the ground station.

Competition

Teams are evaluated on technical design, scientific value, teamwork, and presentation skills. The competition tests all aspects of a real space mission from design to operation.

Our CanSat Project

As the telecommunications subsystem manager, I led the design of our robust communication system that ensured reliable data transmission throughout the mission.

Technical Specifications

Microcontroller

STM32F4 series with real-time operating system for reliable control

Telecommunications

900MHz RF transceiver with custom antenna design for robust link

Payload Sensors

Temperature, pressure, humidity, and air quality sensors for atmospheric analysis

Recovery System

Dual deployment parachute with altitude-triggered release mechanism

3D Model of Our CanSat

This interactive 3D model shows our detailed CAD design created in CATIA, demonstrating the compact integration of all subsystems.

Telecommunications Subsystem

As the telecommunications manager, I designed a robust communication system that could maintain a stable link throughout the mission profile despite various challenges:

  • Custom-designed dipole antenna optimized for our frequency band
  • Error correction protocols to handle signal degradation during descent
  • Redundant transmission paths to prevent data loss
  • Real-time signal strength monitoring and adaptive power adjustment

Mission Objectives

Our CanSat was designed to accomplish several scientific and engineering objectives during its flight:

Atmospheric Analysis

Measure temperature, pressure, and humidity profiles during descent

Precision Landing

Implement controlled descent to land within a target zone

Data Integrity

Ensure complete transmission of all scientific data despite challenging conditions

Our University: Amirkabir University of Technology

Known as Tehran Polytechnic, Amirkabir University of Technology is Iran's oldest and most prestigious technical university. Founded in 1928, it has been at the forefront of engineering education and research in the country.
